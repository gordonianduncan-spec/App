Grammar: The Simple Present Tense

The Simple Present Tense is used for actions that are habits, facts, or things that happen regularly.

How to form it:

  • For most verbs, use the base form: I eat. You eat. We eat. They eat.
  • For "he," "she," and "it," add an "-s" to the end of the verb: He eats. She eats. It eats.

Examples:

  • Fact: The sun rises in the east.
  • Habit: I drink coffee every morning.
  • Regular action: She walks to school.

Essential Vocabulary

Hello

A common greeting, used when you meet someone.

Example: "Hello! How are you today?"

Good

Adjective used to describe something positive or correct.

Example: "That's a very good idea."

Thank you

A polite way to show gratitude.

Example: "Thank you for your help."

Please

Used to make a polite request.

Example: "Please close the door."

Excuse me

Used to politely get someone's attention or ask them to move.

Example: "Excuse me, where is the station?"

Sorry

Used to apologize for something.

Example: "I'm so sorry, I didn't see you."
